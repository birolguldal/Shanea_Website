About CHASM

Community Healing Alliance of Southeast Michigan

Community Healing Alliance of Southeast Michigan (CHASM) is a community-based project that aims to engage and support the mental health of Black, Indigenous, and People of Color (BIPOC) trans adolescents in Southeast Michigan by providing information, resources, and referrals to services for mental health support.


The content and materials found on our website stem from individuals and organizations that have the capacity to provide mental health support specifically to those who have experienced trauma. As such, CHASM functions as a mutual aid source that aims to increase folks’ awareness of trans-affirming, trauma-informed services available to BIPOC adolescents. It also aims to break down barriers that prevent or otherwise limit access to appropriate and timely mental health care by equipping folks with valuable tools while simultaneously offering them guidance as they navigate complex systems of care.

Stigma, Trauma, and Self-Efficacy

🌸


Stigma


Stigma is a negative attitude toward or perception of a person or group of people based on a particular attribute or distinguishing characteristic.


It is a form of discrimination that typically attaches shame or disgrace to the person or thing being stigmatized.

🕊


Trauma

Trauma is an emotional response to an extraordinarily distressing or disturbing experience that can shatter one’s sense of security and sense of self.


As such, it can have a significant impact on a person’s ability to cope and function and can result in extensive physical, psychological, and emotional harm.
Trauma comes in many shapes and forms, but common traumatic events include abuse, assault, death of a loved one, and natural disasters, among others.

🌤


Self-efficacy

Self-efficacy is an individual’s belief in their capacity to execute behaviors that are necessary to accomplish a specific task or succeed in a given situation.


One’s level of self-efficacy can be determined by the extent to which they feel they are able to manage their own motivation and behavior in the social environment. 

 part of the human experience, stigma, trauma and self-efficacy intersect and heavily inform one another.
                     Trauma survivors may experience stigma in response to their trauma history, which has the potential to further traumatize them. 
                     As such, both trauma and stigma may inhibit the development of self-efficacy and diminish one’s belief in their ability to overcome 
                     personal obstacles and take control of their lives.This has significant implications for one’s mental health and emotional well-being,
                      which inherently has an impact on physical health, too.
